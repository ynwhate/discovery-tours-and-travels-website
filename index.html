The dream of flying in the sky just like a bird is as old as human kind. You can make your dream come true with paragliding. This sport is considered to be risky, but the reality is that the safety of practitioners is quite high, especially compared to other adventure disciplines.
Hiking, walking in nature as a recreational activity. Especially among those with sedentary occupations, hiking is a natural exercise that promotes physical fitness, is economical and convenient, and requires no special equipment
Skiing is a recreational activity as well as a competitive sport. The skier attaches long skis, also called runners to specially designed boots and uses them to travel on top of snow.
Rafting, the high-adrenaline sport of navigating a river in an inflatable raft, involves several levels of difficulty, depending on how choppy the river is
The term wildlife safari used to be associated with big-game hunts. Nowadays people no longer “hunt” for wildlife but for observing all creatures great and small, in their natural habitats. This is sightseeing out in the Great Wide Open.
Kayaks have been described as “those smart little, tight little, slight little, light little, thin little, slim little crafts”. It involves travelling on water using a kayak, a narrow boat manoeuvred by a double-blade paddle.
Cycling (also known as bicycling or biking) is the riding of bicycles (also cycles, or bikes) for leisure or as part of competition.
Nothing better sums up the outdoors than the centuries-old human endeavor to scale mighty peaks. Mountaineering involves hiking, climbing, or just simply walking, on hilly or mountainous ground, with the help of technical equipment and support.
